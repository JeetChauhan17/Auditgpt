# Satyam Computer Services Fraud (2009)

## Overview
India's largest corporate fraud. Chairman Ramalinga Raju confessed on January 7, 2009 to systematically inflating revenues, margins, and cash balances over 6 years (2003-2008). Rs 7,136 crore in fictitious cash and bank balances.

## Key Manipulations
1. **Revenue Inflation**: Created ~7,000 fake invoices from non-existent clients. Revenue inflated by 20-25%.
2. **Cash Fabrication**: Showed Rs 5,040 crore cash that did not exist. Forged bank statements.
3. **Employee Ghost Rolls**: 13,000 fictitious employees on payroll. Salaries diverted.
4. **Operating Margin Inflation**: Reported OPM of 24% vs actual ~3%.

## Detectable Signals (in hindsight)
- **DSRI steadily rising**: Days sales in receivables grew from 72 to 105 days (2003-2008). Revenue was booked but cash never came.
- **CFO/Net Income ratio declining**: From 0.85 to 0.15. Profits existed only on paper.
- **Beneish M-Score crossed -1.78 by 2006**: The quantitative model flagged it 3 years before confession.
- **Altman Z-Score declining**: From 3.2 to 1.3 over 5 years despite "growing" revenue.
- **Industry comparison**: IT Services peers (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) had CFO/NI > 0.8 and stable DSRI. Satyam's divergence was glaring.

## Timeline
- 2003: Manipulation begins. Metrics still within normal ranges.
- 2004-2005: DSRI starts climbing. M-Score approaches threshold.
- 2006: M-Score crosses -1.78. CFO/NI drops below 0.5.
- 2007: Clear divergence from industry peers. Cash balance growth doesn't match operations.
- 2008: Attempted Maytas acquisition (to fill the gap with real assets). Board blocked it.
- Jan 2009: Confession.

## Lessons
1. Cash flow analysis is more reliable than reported earnings
2. Industry-relative benchmarking catches what absolute metrics miss
3. Progressive deterioration over 3+ years is a stronger signal than single-year anomalies
