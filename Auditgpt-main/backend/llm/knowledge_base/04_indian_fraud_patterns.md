# Common Financial Fraud Patterns in Indian Companies

## Revenue Manipulation
- **Channel stuffing**: Pushing inventory to distributors at quarter-end with buy-back guarantees
- **Round-tripping**: Revenue through shell companies (promoter-linked entities)
- **Related Party Transactions (RPT)**: Inflated transactions with promoter group companies
- **Bill-and-hold arrangements**: Recording revenue before delivery

## Expense Manipulation
- **Capitalizing operating expenses**: Moving costs from P&L to balance sheet
- **Under-provisioning for bad debts**: Not writing off irrecoverable receivables
- **Extending asset useful life**: Reducing depreciation charges
- **Ghost employees**: Fictitious payroll entries (Satyam pattern)

## Balance Sheet Manipulation
- **Fictitious assets**: Non-existent cash, investments, or receivables
- **Off-balance sheet liabilities**: Hiding debt through subsidiaries
- **Inventory inflation**: Overstating inventory quantities or values
- **Goodwill inflation**: Overpaying for acquisitions to park cash

## Red Flags Specific to Indian Market
1. Promoter pledge > 50% of holdings
2. Frequent auditor changes
3. Delayed filing of quarterly results
4. High related-party transaction volume (> 10% of revenue)
5. Subsidiary structures in tax havens (Mauritius, Cayman)
6. Persistent gap between reported profit and operating cash flow
7. Receivables growing faster than revenue for 3+ consecutive years
8. Unusual cash balance growth without corresponding revenue growth

## Notable Indian Fraud Cases
- **Satyam (2009)**: Revenue and cash fabrication — Rs 7,136 crore
- **DHFL (2019)**: Loan diversion — Rs 34,000 crore
- **IL&FS (2018)**: Debt concealment — Rs 91,000 crore
- **Vakrangee (2018)**: Revenue round-tripping through franchisees
- **Manpasand Beverages (2019)**: Revenue inflation via fictitious distributors
